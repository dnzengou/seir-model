Info & user instructions:

The SEIR model is a compartmental model in which the possible epidemic states of the individuals (agents) are susceptible (S - blue), exposed (E - green), infected (I - red), recovered or removed (R - yellow, sometimes also called resistant). Each of these states correspond to the number of people in each group.
The graphs here show the state transitions for the expected numbers of agents moving over time from S to E, I and R, then eventually back to S (if interventions though not considered here), also illustrating those dying over time. A more detailed description of the model can be found in recent scholar litterature, SEIR and COVID-19 models description. For questions, reach out here.
